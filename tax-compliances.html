Frequently Asked Questions

What is Form 15CA and Form 15CB, and when are they required?
Form 15CA is a declaration form filled out by a person making a remittance to a non-resident or a foreign company. Form 15CB is an official certificate issued by a practicing Chartered Accountant, certifying that the applicable double-taxation avoidance agreement (DTAA) provisions and tax deduction rates (TDS) have been complied with under Section 195.
Why is GSTR-2B matching critical for GST compliance?
Input Tax Credit (ITC) can only be claimed if your supplier has uploaded their invoice and it is displayed in your GSTR-2B statement. GSA runs automated monthly matching checks to detect missing supplier uploads, preventing you from losing tax credits or facing interest penalties.
Does GSA represent clients during tax audits and disputes?
Yes. We assist in responding to tax department notices, preparing detailed compliance submissions, and representing your business in hearings before Income Tax and GST assessment authorities.
