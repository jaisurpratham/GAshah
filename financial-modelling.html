Financial Modelling

Data-driven mathematical models to project corporate performance, value assets, and evaluate business strategies.

Structuring Numbers for Strategic Decisions

A corporate financial model is a mathematical representation of your business operations. It acts as an essential tool for predicting cash requirements, evaluating investments, and negotiating with financial backers. GSA designs customized Excel financial models that map your historical performance to future growth projections, translating business ideas into concrete data.

We work in close coordination with your team to collect data, outline reasonable assumptions, build the core model sheets, and stress-test the outputs across varying market conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a 3-statement financial model, and why is it important?
A 3-statement model dynamically links the Profit & Loss statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow statement together. When you modify a revenue assumption, it automatically calculates the impact on your cash reserves, corporate liabilities, and tax obligations, providing an integrated view of your financial state.
How does GSA validate the accuracy of its financial models?
We implement strict Excel audit practices, using built-in balance checks (e.g., verifying that the assets match liabilities on the balance sheet for all forecast years). Every model is peer-reviewed by a second senior partner before delivery to ensure formulas are free of circular references.
Can you build models to support venture funding rounds?
Yes. We design investment-ready models that include Capitalization (Cap) tables showing dilution, discounted cash flow (DCF) valuations, Internal Rate of Return (IRR) indicators, and payback periods that institutional VCs and banks expect.
