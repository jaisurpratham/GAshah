Statutory Audit Services

Independent financial audits ensuring compliance with Indian accounting standards and the Companies Act 2013.

Independent Verification & Absolute Compliance

A statutory audit is a legally required review of an organization's financial records. The primary objective is to verify the accuracy of the financial statements, ensuring that they represent a true and fair view of the entity's financial health, and comply with the applicable provisions of the **Indian Companies Act 2013** and relevant accounting standards.

At GSA, we perform statutory audits primarily for SME corporate clients, including private limited companies. Our audit process is designed to combine legal compliance with actionable recommendations to improve internal processes.

Our Auditing Steps

We implement a structured onboarding methodology to ensure a smooth transition of your accounting operations:

01

Planning

Determining audit materiality levels and identifying potential risk accounts.

02

03

Scope of Statutory Audits

Audit Module Core Tasks & Deliverables Filing Requirement

What is IFC (Internal Financial Control) reporting?
IFC refers to the policies and procedures adopted by the company to ensure orderly conduct of business, safeguarding of assets, prevention of fraud, accuracy of books, and timely preparation of financial reports. Auditors must verify if these controls exist and operate effectively.
What are the deadlines for statutory audit filings in India?
The statutory audit must be completed before the company's Annual General Meeting (AGM), which must be held within 6 months of the close of the financial year (typically by September 30th). The audited accounts (Form AOC-4) must be filed with the ROC within 30 days of the AGM.
