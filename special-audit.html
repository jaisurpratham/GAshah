Frequently Asked Questions

What is a management audit, and when is it required?
A management audit is a systematic evaluation of an organization's management decisions, operational structures, and resource utilizations. It is usually initiated by board directors or major stakeholders during restructuring to find inefficiencies in leadership structures, communication, or process planning.
How does financial due diligence support mergers and acquisitions?
Before you acquire a business or purchase a stake, you must verify the accuracy of the target's financial claims. GSA performs due diligence audits to verify their asset valuations, detect unrecorded corporate liabilities, review historical tax audits, and identify potential risks that could affect the deal price.
What is a special purpose audit?
A special purpose audit is scoped around a specific set of financial accounts, contracts, or transactions rather than the entire corporate financial statements. Examples include auditing joint venture revenue sharing, verifying royalty computations, or investigating specific transaction discrepancies.
