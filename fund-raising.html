Data room security is critical during transaction due diligence. GSA coordinates the creation of Virtual Deal Rooms (VDR) with access permissions, watermarked documents, and blocked download capabilities to protect sensitive corporate assets.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Virtual Data Room (VDR) and why is it used?
A VDR is a secure online repository used for storing and sharing sensitive corporate documents (such as financial models, tax records, and employee contracts) during M&A or funding due diligence. It prevents unauthorized file downloads, tracks who views documents, and secures trade secrets.
Should my company raise debt or equity?
Debt is cheaper and preserves founder equity, but requires regular cash flows to service interest. Equity is more expensive and dilutes ownership, but has no repayment schedule and brings strategic investor networks. GSA runs cash diagnostics to recommend the optimal ratio based on your runway.
How does GSA help analyze investor term sheets?
We translate legal term sheet clauses into financial impacts, analyzing liquidation preferences (e.g., participating vs. non-participating), anti-dilution provisions, veto rights, board seats, and drag-along rights to protect founder interests.
