Internal Audit Services

Objective assessments of your internal control frameworks, governance procedures, and risk management systems.

Strengthening Internal Controls and Governance

Internal auditing is an independent and objective assurance activity. Our primary objective is to evaluate the effectiveness and efficiency of your business operations, identify vulnerabilities in your accounting controls, protect corporate assets, and provide clear recommendations to improve governance processes.

At GSA, we perform internal audits for SME clients, focusing on private limited companies. Our approach goes beyond simple verification of vouchers; we analyze your operational workflows to help leadership prevent cash leakages and optimize asset utilization.

Our Auditing Approach

We implement a structured onboarding methodology to ensure a smooth transition of your accounting operations:

01

Control Map

Mapping existing operational divisions, transaction approval limits, and IT systems.

02

Risk Check

Evaluating vulnerability exposure: identifying cash leakages, billing errors, or fraud risks.

03

Execution

testing ledger balances, physically auditing inventory, and validating asset registers.

04

Monitoring

Reporting findings to the audit committee and tracking implementation status quarterly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a statutory audit and an internal audit?
A statutory audit is legally mandated, retrospective, and focuses on verifying if the financial statements represent a true and fair view for external stakeholders (investors, banks, ROC). An internal audit is voluntary (mostly), forward-looking, and focuses on testing internal controls, risk management, and operational efficiency to help the management improve processes.
Is an internal audit mandatory for private limited companies in India?
Under Section 138 of the Companies Act 2013, internal audit is mandatory for private limited companies if their turnover exceeds Rs. 200 crore during the preceding financial year, or if their outstanding loans/borrowings from banks exceed Rs. 100 crore. Many SMEs voluntarily choose internal audits to safeguard operations.
How does GSA help prevent inventory or cash leakages?
We run transactional audits, testing for dual approval overrides, matching supplier purchase rates against master agreements, auditing credit note issues, and conducting surprise physical verifications of inventory, ensuring all discrepancies are flagged.
